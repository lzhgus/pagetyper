Typing as a Way of Reading

Reading slowly changes the texture of attention. When each sentence has to pass through the hands, the reader notices rhythm, punctuation, repeated ideas, and the small choices that make a paragraph feel clear or heavy.

A typing layer should not fight the page. It should find the main article, preserve the reading context, and give immediate feedback without turning the browser into a noisy training app. The best version feels quiet, close to the text, and easy to leave.

This sample page gives PageTyper enough article structure to test detection, highlighting, mistakes, backspace behavior, progress, speed, and accuracy in a normal browser tab.
